Quick Start Guide

From zero to your first 3D architectural model in under 10 minutes. No installation, no account — just a browser.

1. Download & Open

1

Download the file

Click the Download button on the main site. Save aiarchitekV6.html anywhere on your computer — your Desktop works fine.

2

Open it in your browser

Double-click the file. It opens directly in Chrome, Firefox, Edge, or Safari. No install needed. The entire app is self-contained in that one file.

3

You're ready

The app loads instantly. You'll see the toolbar on the left, the 3D viewport in the center, and the settings panel on the right.

Tip: Works 100% offline after download. No internet connection required once the file is on your computer.

2. The Interface

AI-Architek has three main areas:

◧

Left Panel — Tools

All draw and transform tools live here, organized into sections: Transform, Views, Elements, and Camera Views. Click a tool to activate it, or use its keyboard shortcut.

▣

Center — 3D Viewport

Your modeling canvas. Left-drag to orbit, right-drag to pan, scroll to zoom. Press 3 for perspective, 4 for top-down plan view.

◨

Right Panel — Settings

Controls for grid size, lighting (ambient, sun, fill), shadows, sky, and solar position. Changes apply in real time.

3. Draw Your First Wall

1

Press W to activate the Wall tool

Or click Wall in the left panel. The cursor changes to indicate you're in draw mode.

2

Click to place the first point

Click anywhere in the viewport to set the wall's start point. You'll see a preview line following your cursor.

3

Click again to set the end point

Walls chain automatically — keep clicking to add more connected wall segments. Double-click or press Esc to finish.

4

Add a door or window

Press D for Door or I for Window, then click on a wall face to auto-place it.

5. Select & Edit Objects

Click any object to select it (it glows cyan). Ctrl+Click to add to the selection. Drag a box around multiple objects to select them all.

With an object selected you can:

M

Move

3-step: select the object → click a base point → click the destination.

K

Copy

Same 3-step as Move but leaves the original in place and creates a copy.

Del

Delete

Press Delete or click the Delete button in the top toolbar to remove selected objects.

Undo: Press Ctrl+Z to undo and Ctrl+Y to redo. Multi-object moves all reverse together.

6. Save Your Work

Click Save or Save As... in the top toolbar. Your scene saves as a .json file that you can reload at any time with the Open button. Everything is saved — objects, lighting, camera views, and settings.

Good habit: Save often with Save As to keep versioned backups, e.g. myproject-v1.json, myproject-v2.json.
