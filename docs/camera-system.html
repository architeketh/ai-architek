Camera System

Save unlimited named camera views, reposition them live with draggable handles, and cycle lens types — all new in V6.

Saving a Camera View

1

Set up your view

Orbit, pan, and zoom the viewport to exactly the angle you want to save. Use V (Set Cam) to click an eye position and a target point for precision.

2

Type a name

In the Camera Views panel at the bottom of the left sidebar, type a name in the text box (e.g. "Entry View" or "South Elevation").

3

Click + Save

Click the + Save button. The view is stored with its name, position, target, and current lens setting.

4

Recall it anytime

Click the ▶ play arrow next to any saved view to jump back to it instantly.

Frustum Cones

Each saved camera displays as a yellow wireframe cone in the 3D scene, showing exactly where the camera is and what it can see.

👁

Show / Hide cones

Click Cam Cones in the Views panel to toggle all frustum cones on or off. Use the per-camera eye icon to hide individual cameras.

🟡

Drag the yellow sphere (eye)

Grab the yellow sphere at the cone's tip to reposition the camera's eye point live in the scene.

🟠

Drag the orange sphere (target)

Grab the orange sphere to re-aim where the camera is looking. The cone redraws in real time.

🔍

Double-click to cycle lens

Double-click either sphere to cycle through the three lens settings: Wide 90° → Normal 50° → Tele 20°. The cone shape updates immediately.

Lens Settings

You can also click the lens button in the camera list to cycle the lens for that camera. The three options are:

W

Wide — 90°

Wide angle. Shows a large field of view. Good for interior shots and tight spaces.

N

Normal — 50°

Natural perspective, similar to the human eye. Good default for most exterior views.

T

Tele — 20°

Telephoto / compressed perspective. Good for distant elevations and presentation shots.

Plan view footprint: In Plan (top) view, each camera shows a direction arrow and a horizontal FOV outline on the floor so you can see coverage from above. Cones scale automatically with zoom level.
