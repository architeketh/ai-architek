Lighting & Sky

Configure scene lighting, real-world solar position, and a sky backdrop — all from the right panel.

Lighting Controls

Ambient Base fill light applied equally to all surfaces. Raise it to brighten the whole scene without shadows.
Sun Intensity Strength of the directional sun light. Controls how bright lit faces appear and how strong shadows are cast.
Sun Color Color of the sun light. Automatically shifts warm orange at sunrise/sunset and cool white at noon when using Solar Position.
Fill Intensity Secondary soft light from the opposite direction to the sun. Reduces harsh shadow contrast on the dark side of buildings.
Shadows Toggle shadow casting on or off. Shadows show realistically based on the sun's current position.

Solar Position

The Solar Position tool calculates real sun angles for any location, month, and time of day — so your shadows are architecturally accurate.

Country Choose from 14 available countries and regions.
Region Narrows to a state or sub-region for more precise latitude/longitude.
Month Sets the time of year. Sun angle changes significantly between winter and summer.
Time of Day Drag the slider to move through the day. The sun's azimuth and elevation update in real time. The sun turns off at night automatically.
Reading the result: Below the controls you'll see the calculated Elevation (how high the sun is above the horizon) and Azimuth (compass direction the sun is coming from) displayed live.

Sky System

The Sky system adds a background behind your scene — either a solid color gradient or a full panoramic image.

Sky On/Off Toggle the sky backdrop on or off. When off, the viewport background is plain dark.
Sky Color Color of the upper sky. Click the color swatch to open the color picker.
Horizon Color Color at the horizon line. Blends with Sky Color to create a gradient sky.
Upload Sky Image Upload any JPG or PNG as a sky image. Equirectangular panoramas wrap as a full 360° sky dome. The image also appears as a backdrop in Front, Left, Back, and Right elevation views.
Clear Image Removes the uploaded image and returns to the color gradient sky.
Tip: Free equirectangular panoramas are available on sites like Polyhaven (polyhaven.com) — download any HDRI or JPG panorama and upload it for a realistic sky backdrop.
